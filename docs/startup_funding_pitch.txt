DRISHYAM AI - STARTUP FUNDING PITCH
Version: Investor + government + strategic partner narrative

ONE-LINE POSITIONING
DRISHYAM AI is a citizen-first fraud interception, intelligence, and recovery platform for India that connects the moment of scam exposure to real operational response across bank, telecom, police, and government workflows.


1. PROBLEM STATEMENT
India's digital economy is scaling faster than its fraud response layer.

As UPI, mobile access, and digital onboarding grow, cyber fraud is becoming:
- faster
- more personalized
- more voice-led
- more cross-channel
- more damaging for first-time and low-literacy users

Current response is fragmented:
- citizens receive alerts but no unified response path
- banks receive limited context after the fraud is underway
- telecom systems detect abuse but do not own the victim journey
- police often receive incomplete evidence
- recovery is painful and uncoordinated
- feature-phone and low-bandwidth users remain underserved

This creates a large trust gap in Digital India.

We believe the next category winner in cyber safety is not just a detector.
It is an orchestration layer.


2. USER - KISKE LIYE AUR KYU
Our user base is multi-sided.

A. Citizen side
- smartphone users who face scam calls, fake UPI requests, phishing, impersonation, and social engineering
- senior citizens and families who need simpler, trust-first safety experiences
- users in smaller cities and low-bandwidth environments who cannot rely on heavy app workflows

B. Institutional side
- banks and payment partners that need earlier fraud intelligence and structured case data
- telecom partners that need scam-linked signal intelligence and actionable reporting
- police / cyber cells that need evidence-ready complaints, not fragmented narration
- government programs that need scalable citizen protection tools and measurable outcomes

Why they need us:
- citizen wants protection and recovery
- bank wants early risk signal and less fraud leakage
- telecom wants scam network visibility
- police wants better evidence and faster triage
- government wants safer digital adoption at scale


3. SOLUTION
DRISHYAM AI combines five layers into one operational system:

1. Interception layer
- AI honeypot for suspicious calls and scam conversations

2. Detection layer
- UPI ID, QR, and scam-message risk checks

3. Bharat access layer
- low-bandwidth and feature-phone friendly reporting flows

4. Response layer
- structured routing to bank, telecom, police, and command dashboard

5. Recovery and resilience layer
- evidence packaging, next-step guidance, recovery support, and drills

This is important because most alternatives solve only one of these layers.
We solve the full citizen-to-agency loop.


4. KEY FEATURES
Core product modules:

- AI Scam Handoff
  Lets AI take over suspicious voice or chat interactions, keeping the citizen safe while collecting evidence.

- Voice / Video Trace
  Extracts scam entities such as caller numbers, VPAs, urgency patterns, institutions, and coercive tactics.

- UPI Armor
  Checks UPI IDs, QR codes, and scam messages and can route suspicious findings into structured action.

- Bharat Layer
  Supports 1930-style and USSD-like reporting journeys for low-bandwidth users.

- Recovery Companion
  Helps users with case follow-up, evidence bundle creation, and recovery workflow guidance.

- Drill Center
  Builds preventive behavior through guided scam resilience training.

- Agency Dashboard
  Gives police, bank, telecom, and administrators live case views, routing context, and auditability.

- Audit and Action Trail
  Every key action can be logged, reviewed, and linked to a case workflow.


5. VISION
Our vision is to become India's fraud response operating layer.

The long-term goal is:
- not just to detect scams
- but to reduce successful fraud completion
- reduce time to response
- improve evidence quality
- improve victim recovery pathways
- increase trust in digital payments and digital public infrastructure

In 5 years, DRISHYAM AI can become:
- a B2G public safety stack
- a B2B risk-routing platform
- a citizen trust layer embedded into financial and telecom channels


6. MARKET SPACE
Market timing is strong.

Reference points:
- RBI Payment System Indicators, March 2025: UPI processed about 185.9 billion transactions worth roughly Rs 260.6 lakh crore in FY 2024-25.
- RBI domestic payment fraud data for March 2025 recorded 1.84 lakh fraud cases worth Rs 291 crore.
- TRAI 2024-25 yearbook: India had 1,156.99 million wireless subscribers and 939.51 million wireless data subscribers by March 2025.

Implications:
- India's digital payments market is massive.
- Even a small reduction in fraud incidence or loss has material economic value.
- The gap between total wireless users and data users leaves a large low-bandwidth safety segment open.

Practical TAM framing:
- citizen safety and anti-fraud tooling for banks, telecoms, fintechs, and public digital channels
- fraud intelligence and workflow software for institutions
- embedded trust and recovery services around high-risk consumer interactions

This is a large and growing digital trust market, not a narrow cybersecurity niche.


7. COMPETITOR
We do not see one single direct competitor today. We see adjacent categories.

1. Caller reputation apps
- example: Truecaller
- strong on spam identification
- weak on full case orchestration, citizen recovery, evidence, and low-bandwidth routing

2. Government reporting systems
- example: 1930 / NCRP / I4C ecosystem
- essential national rails
- strong on complaint intake and escalation
- not designed as a proactive AI interception and resilience layer

3. Enterprise verification / fraud vendors
- example categories: identity verification, device intelligence, threat intelligence, risk scoring
- useful for institutions
- usually not citizen-first, multi-stakeholder, or Bharat-access focused

4. Consumer anti-scam tools
- browser or device-side detectors
- useful for alerts
- limited on institutional workflow integration

Our moat is orchestration:
- citizen UX + institutional routing
- real-time interception + post-event recovery
- India-specific, multilingual, low-bandwidth-aware workflows
- cross-stakeholder product design instead of single-point detection
